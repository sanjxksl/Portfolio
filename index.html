I build products by connecting what's technically possible with what users actually need. I believe good products come from understanding technical limits, user behavior, business goals, and finding solutions that work within all of them. I write Python, design in Figma, and lead stakeholder conversations because product work requires moving between all of them.

Currently at Rotman's Management Analytics program, learning to use data to make better product decisions. Before this, I spent four years in India studying mechanical engineering and discovering I loved understanding why people wanted things more than how things worked. That curiosity led me through product design, then analytics, and now I'm figuring out how they all fit together.

I overthink everything‚Äîit's both my superpower and my curse. I'll spend hours mapping out edge cases, user flows, and failure modes before writing a single line of code. Some call it analysis paralysis; I call it making sure I'm solving the right problem. The overthinking thing? It's a feature, not a bug.

When I'm not building products or analyzing data, I'm either writing poetry for friends (used to publish, now I just write for people I care about), organizing my life in Notion with probably too many databases, or finding new coffee shops in Toronto where I can sit with a book and just think.
