I'm currently at Rotman's Management Analytics program, learning to turn data into product decisions. I write Python, build ML models, and design in Figma because product work means moving between all of them. Right now, I'm figuring out how to use analytics not just to measure what happened, but to understand what should happen next.

I spent four years studying mechanical engineering in India, thinking I'd design machines. Somewhere along the way, I realized I cared more about why people wanted things than how things worked. That led me to product design, then to data analytics, and now I'm trying to connect all of it. The path wasn't linear, and I definitely didn't have it figured out at 18.

I build products by asking questions first. Every project starts with understanding the problem space before touching code. It takes longer upfront, but I'd rather spend time thinking through edge cases than fixing broken assumptions later.

Outside of work, I move between creative outlets that keep me grounded. What matters most is finding ways to think differently, whether that's through movement, words, or just sitting with an idea until it makes sense.
